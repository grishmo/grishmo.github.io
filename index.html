Grishmo - summer

This website is supposed to document things and instances that I feel are important. Including experiences related to my career, growing up in India and learning different languages.

I intend to make this my new Notes website, as time and again, I do see the need of creating one as such. The main reason to do this is not just to keep a record of things that I come across, but also to have a space to share these with others through a simple url. Certainly, a pre-built website like Wordpress or Blogger would do the job fine. But sometimes I like to be more in control, and have complete knowledge of my things. This site is not just a collection of pages, but more. Nonetheless, that 'more' is only accessible to me, the admin of this website.

Train Models

Probably my first notebook here would be on train models. I have been thinking of putting my engineering knowledge to application, and one good place I found is to try to design and 3-D print trains and run them on rails. After having searched a bit on the internet, I decided that the models would be upon the Lego scale - the so-called L-Gauge. The designs ought to be made using Fusion 360 on my computer, and then 3-D printed out of plastic. As for the tracks, they may be bought in quite some numbers, although I probably would design and print some, just to check which is cheaper. The wagons could come from a few Lego sets as well as designed and printed.
Here I've started the notebook.

A Lego train setup at the Americas. I do not intend to make many models, but definitely some. Maybe also have some crossing tracks and computer controlled management of the trains. The computer controlling could be done using Python and arduino boards.

The notebook on this subject is expected to contain the design and manufacture procedures of the models. Including the research, calculations, drawings and manufacturing methodology. It could be helpful to have this as a future reference and may also be helpful to others.

A very big Lego train setup. A train traverses a big part of the setup, with a camera attached to it to show where it is headed. The setup spans through much of the ground floor of the house, the front part of the house, and through many trees.
